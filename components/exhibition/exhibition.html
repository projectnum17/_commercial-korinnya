exhibition 13.03.2022

More

With the aim of forming a new community, Roots collected the works of twenty-five photographers. In their works, we noticed not a dry reportage shot, but a personal interaction with the experience and reflected reality. The exhibition features works created after February 24.

All authors sent one photo that could best represent their practice. This selection demonstrates a cross-section of contemporary Ukrainian documentary photography. For us, the war is not a headline, it is a living phenomenon that we look at through the camera lens. The war in the photographs of the selected authors is represented not only literally, but also by the images it left its mark on.

Kiyv

During the week of the exhibition, at least 10 thousand people learned about "Roots" on social media, more than 200 people saw the photos in the Avangarden Gallery, and about 10 thousand hryvnias were raised, which almost paid for the printing of the photos. On the first day, a photo by Petro Chekal was sold, and half of the proceeds went to charity. Then the exhibition went to Lviv and was held at the Dzyga Cultural Art Center.

For the first time, we were able to prepare postcards with photos of the photos and a qr code with a link to the author.

About 200 people also visited the exhibition.

Our exhibition was visited by Polish documentary photographers, one of whom is a professor at the Institute of Photography and Multimedia in Lodz, one of the most prestigious photography schools in Europe. The next stop was the city of Odesa

The exhibition was held in the artists' house on Torhova Street 2, near the sea and the seaport. More people visited this place, about 400-500 people, and it lasted for two weeks. We were able to prepare postcards at the opening, and people took about 100 of them with them and left us donations.

In Odesa, however, it was the most difficult because blackouts began, and on the day the exhibition curator Taras Fedorenko arrived, kamikaze drones flew over Odesa. That's why in our promo for the exhibition in Odesa we showed that we were bringing light to the gallery, along with the photographs."
