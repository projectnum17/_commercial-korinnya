About

Starting as a volunteer at the Lviv railway station a documentarian in Kyiv and his hometown - Borodyanka, he learns about the war and transmits it through photography from different perspectives.

George studied the direction of the Russian army's advance in Chernihiv region and later had expeditions to Kharkiv, Mykolaiv, Donetsk and Luhansk regions. This experience gave impetus to the creation of the Ukrainian documentary-reflexive photo association "Korinnya" which aims to unite and popularize Ukrainian photographers. George still goes on expeditions to the front and near-front zones.

George is a freelance reporter and collaborates with photo agencies: Associated Press and European Pressphoto Agency, through which his photos have been published in New York Times, The Guardian, The Journal, Journalisten, Clarin, San Francisco Chronicle, Usmission OSCE, Polskie Radio, Outlook, Los Angeles Times, Novaya Gazeta: Europe, etc. He also exhibits his works in group and individual exhibitions.

is works have also been published in: The Ukrainian (ua), NZL (ua), Ukrainer (ua) Center for Strategic Communications and Information Security (ua).  

"At 15 when I went to study in another city I put my old mother's Canon camera in my bag and since then I became interested in photography. Most of all I was interested in life, shadows, light, and geometry. "There is a game called" capture the moment. "I photographed everything over time I began to pay attention to individual ordinary people in ordinary towns and villages talked to them and took pictures from under the belly. With the beginning of the full-scale invasion I volunteered for a week at the Lviv railway station and went to Kyiv taking pictures of the daily life that raged there. When Kyiv region was liberated on the first day he went to Borodyanka to myrelatives and then to Chernihiv, Kharkiv, Mykolaiv, Kherson, and finally to the Lugansk and Donetsk oblast where i took photos of the fighting.”
